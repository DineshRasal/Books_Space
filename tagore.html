Rabindranath Tagore

Rabindranath Tagore (1861–1941) was an Indian poet, writer, philosopher, painter, and Nobel laureate in Literature (1913). He became the first non-European to win the Nobel Prize in Literature for his collection Gitanjali.

Tagore reshaped Bengali literature and music, and his works are deeply rooted in Indian culture while carrying universal humanistic themes.

He also composed the national anthems of India (Jana Gana Mana) and Bangladesh (Amar Shonar Bangla).

Famous Works

  • Gitanjali (1910)
  • The Home and the World (1916)
  • Gora (1910)
  • Kabuliwala (1892)
  • Fireflies (1916)
