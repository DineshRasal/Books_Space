Leo Tolstoy

Leo Tolstoy (1828–1910) was a Russian novelist, philosopher, and moral thinker, considered one of the greatest authors of all time.

His works are known for their deep psychological insight, realistic portrayal of Russian society, and profound exploration of human morality, spirituality, and non-violence.

Tolstoy’s influence extended beyond literature into social and political thought, inspiring figures like Mahatma Gandhi and Martin Luther King Jr..

Famous Works

  • War and Peace (1869)
  • Anna Karenina (1877)
  • The Death of Ivan Ilyich (1886)
  • Resurrection (1899)
  • A Confession (1882)
