William Shakespeare

William Shakespeare (1564–1616) was an English playwright, poet, and actor, widely regarded as the greatest writer in the English language and the world’s pre-eminent dramatist.

Often called the "Bard of Avon," Shakespeare’s works include 39 plays, 154 sonnets, and two long narrative poems. His plays have been translated into every major language and are performed more often than those of any other playwright.

His themes of love, power, betrayal, ambition, and the human condition remain timeless and continue to influence literature and theatre worldwide.
