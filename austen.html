Jane Austen

Jane Austen (1775–1817) was an English novelist known for her keen social commentary, sharp wit, and exploration of women’s lives in early 19th-century England.

Her novels often focused on themes of love, marriage, class, and morality, capturing the nuances of relationships and society with humor and intelligence.

Today, Austen is celebrated as one of the most important figures in English literature, with her works adapted into countless films, TV shows, and stage productions.

Famous Works

  • Pride and Prejudice (1813)
  • Sense and Sensibility (1811)
  • Emma (1815)
  • Mansfield Park (1814)
  • Persuasion (1817)
