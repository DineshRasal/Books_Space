George Orwell

George Orwell (1903–1950), born Eric Arthur Blair, was an English novelist, essayist, journalist, and critic, best known for his works that explore themes of totalitarianism, social injustice, and truth.

His writing style is known for its clarity, directness, and political insight. Orwell’s works remain highly influential in literature, politics, and popular culture.

Terms like “Big Brother,” “Orwellian,” and “Thought Police” come from his works and are still widely used today.

Famous Works

  • Animal Farm (1945)
  • Nineteen Eighty-Four (1949)
  • Homage to Catalonia (1938)
  • Down and Out in Paris and London (1933)
  • The Road to Wigan Pier (1937)
