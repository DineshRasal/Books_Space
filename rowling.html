J.K. Rowling

J.K. Rowling (Joanne Rowling) is a British author best known for writing the Harry Potter fantasy series, which has sold more than 500 million copies worldwide and been translated into over 80 languages.

Born on 31 July 1965 in Yate, Gloucestershire, England, Rowling came up with the idea of Harry Potter while delayed on a train from Manchester to London in 1990.

The seven-book Harry Potter series became a global phenomenon, leading to movies, theme parks, and a cultural legacy. Rowling has also written crime novels under the pen name Robert Galbraith.

Famous Works

  • Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (1997)
  • Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets (1998)
  • Harry Potter and the Prisoner of Azkaban (1999)
  • The Casual Vacancy (2012)
  • The Cormoran Strike series (as Robert Galbraith)
