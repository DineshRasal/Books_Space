J.R.R. Tolkien

J.R.R. Tolkien (John Ronald Reuel Tolkien) was an English writer, poet, philologist, and academic, best known as the author of the high-fantasy works The Hobbit and The Lord of the Rings.

Born on 3 January 1892 in Bloemfontein, South Africa, Tolkien later became a professor at Oxford University, specializing in English literature and languages.

His works introduced detailed fictional worlds with rich histories, languages, and cultures, establishing him as the "Father of Modern Fantasy Literature".

Famous Works

  • The Hobbit (1937)
  • The Lord of the Rings trilogy (1954–1955)
  • The Silmarillion (1977, posthumous)
  • Unfinished Tales (1980, posthumous)
  • The Children of Húrin (2007, posthumous)
