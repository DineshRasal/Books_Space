Charles Dickens

Charles Dickens (1812–1870) was an English novelist, journalist, and social critic, widely regarded as one of the greatest writers of the Victorian era.

His works vividly depicted the struggles of the poor and working-class in 19th-century England, while also offering memorable characters and powerful storytelling.

Dickens’s novels remain popular worldwide and have been adapted into numerous films, TV shows, and stage plays.

Famous Works

  • A Tale of Two Cities (1859)
  • Great Expectations (1861)
  • Oliver Twist (1838)
  • David Copperfield (1850)
  • Bleak House (1853)
