Ernest Hemingway

Ernest Hemingway (1899–1961) was an American novelist, short-story writer, and journalist, known for his concise and straightforward prose style.

He was part of the “Lost Generation” of writers and became one of the most influential authors of the 20th century. His adventurous life—from war correspondent to big-game hunter—inspired much of his work.

Hemingway won the Nobel Prize in Literature in 1954 and the Pulitzer Prize in 1953 for The Old Man and the Sea.

Famous Works

  • The Old Man and the Sea (1952)
  • A Farewell to Arms (1929)
  • For Whom the Bell Tolls (1940)
  • The Sun Also Rises (1926)
  • To Have and Have Not (1937)
