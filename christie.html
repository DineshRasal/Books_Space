Agatha Christie

Agatha Christie (1890–1976) was an English writer, best known for her detective novels, short stories, and plays. She is often called the "Queen of Crime" and is the best-selling fiction author of all time.

Christie created famous detectives such as Hercule Poirot and Miss Marple, whose investigations captivated millions of readers worldwide.

Her works have sold over 2 billion copies, translated into more than 100 languages, and continue to inspire adaptations in film, television, and theatre.

Famous Works

  • The Mysterious Affair at Styles (1920)
  • Murder on the Orient Express (1934)
  • The Murder of Roger Ackroyd (1926)
  • And Then There Were None (1939)
  • Death on the Nile (1937)
