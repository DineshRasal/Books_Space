Fyodor Dostoevsky

Fyodor Dostoevsky (1821–1881) was a Russian novelist, short story writer, essayist, and journalist, widely regarded as one of the greatest psychological novelists in world literature.

His works explore themes of morality, free will, existentialism, faith, and the human psyche, often delving into the struggles of individuals facing poverty, injustice, and inner conflict.

Dostoevsky’s deep exploration of the human condition influenced philosophers, psychologists, and writers worldwide.

Famous Works

  • Crime and Punishment (1866)
  • The Brothers Karamazov (1880)
  • The Idiot (1869)
  • Demons (1872)
  • Notes from Underground (1864)
